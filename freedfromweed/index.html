Clear the Records of Marijuana-Only Offenders

We are petitioning the City of Philadelphia to automatically expunge or seal criminal records of those serving sentences for only marijuana-related offenses.

By following the example set by municipalties like San Francisco, Seattle, and Kansas City, we can take one small step towards healing communities ravaged by the War on Drugs. This won't make up for the decades of damage and trauma inflicted on minority communities—but it's a good start.

Marijuana makes billions for companies while arresting thousands.

As of today, marijuana is legal in some form in 32 states, with many more soon to follow. Every day, it seems like federal legalization is an inevitable future. And legalization is starting to turn a profit—literally. Legal marijuana was worth an astounding $7.6 billion in 2016, and it's been growing rapidly every year since.

While this is great for the economy, it’s terrible for human decency.

People who have had their lives turned upside down by marijuana convictions are forced to sit in prison while watching entire new industries pop up to profit from the very activity they were convicted for.

Additionally, blacks are arrested for marijuana possession a staggering four times more than whites, even though usage is around the same. These convictions strip citizens of voting, housing, employment opportunities. This is a civil rights violation of the highest order, and has been destroying communities for generations.
